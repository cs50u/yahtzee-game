How to Play

Yahtzee Solitaire: A strategic dice game where players maximize scores through skillful choices and dice combinations.

Gameplay

  1. Start your turn: Click the "Roll Dice" button. You can roll the dice up to three times per turn. 13 turns per game.
  2. Lock and unlock dice: After rolling, you can click on any die to lock or unlock them. Locked dice will not change in the next roll.
  3. Choose your action: After each roll, you can either roll the dice again or score a category. If you've already rolled three times, you must score a category.
  4. Score a category: Click an empty cell in the scorecard to score your current dice roll for that category. Each category can only be scored once.
  5. End of the game: The game ends when all categories have been scored. Your grand total score is the sum of all category scores.

Scoring

Your score depends on the category and the current dice roll:

  • Aces to Sixes: The sum of all dice showing the respective number.
  • Three of a Kind: If at least three dice show the same number, your score is the sum of all dice.
  • Four of a Kind: If at least four dice show the same number, your score is the sum of all dice.
  • Full House: Score 25 points if you have three dice showing one number and two dice showing another number (e.g., 3-3-3-6-6).
  • Small Straight: Score 30 points if you have four dice showing consecutive numbers (e.g., 1-2-3-4).
  • Large Straight: Score 40 points if you have five dice showing consecutive numbers (e.g., 1-2-3-4-5).
  • Chance: Your score is the sum of all dice.
  • Yahtzee: Score 50 points if all dice show the same number. Score an additional 100 points for each subsequent Yahtzee (Yahtzee Bonus).

Special Rules

  1. Yahtzee Bonus: Score an additional 100 points for each subsequent Yahtzee after the first.
  2. Joker Rules (Multiple Yahtzees): If you roll a Yahtzee but have already filled the Yahtzee box with a score of 50 or 0, it's time to use the Joker rules. You still select a category as usual, but with some exceptions:
    • If the number corresponding to your Yahtzee is not yet marked in the Upper Section: you must mark it there.
    • If the corresponding number in the Upper Section is already marked: you can score the roll as a Full House, Small Straight, or Large Straight (even if it doesn't meet the usual requirements) for 25, 30, or 40 points respectively. This is when the Yahtzee acts as a "Joker". You can also score the roll as a Three of a Kind, Four of a Kind, or Chance, using the sum of the dice as usual.
    • If the corresponding number in the Upper Section and all relevant Lower Section categories are already filled: you must mark a 0 in an unused Upper Section box.
